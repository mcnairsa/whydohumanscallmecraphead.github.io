Hosting is...

... providing a home for your killer app. If you want the world to know about your killer app but don't want to give the world access your computer (and you really don't want to do that) then you need to find a home for it with a 'hosting provider'. There are different types of hosting service.

  • Shared hosting: like sharing a flat ['appartment'] which is OK till your flatmate has a bunch of friends round and they're up partying all night when you have work the next day. Other users can gobble up resources on the host machine or server. Your users' experience of your site will suffer as a result.
  • Dedicated hosting: you have no flatmates to worry about but you pay all the rent and bills. Generally more expensive but you have more contol over the server.
  • Cloud hosting: more like a hotel or renting a room through AirBnB: you pick what space you need and what facilities you need. this can vary from a room with a bed and you bring your own bed linen to a fully serviced room where you can just turn up and get your head down. There are different models of cloud computing including:
    • Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS): you get the barebones of the hardware but have control over things like what o/s and dbms it runs.
    • Platform as a Service (PaaS): the cloud provider takes responsibility for configuration and provides everything you need to run your application.
    • Function as a Service (FaaS): breaks the application down into its separate function and runs them in response to events. FaaS is itself a form of serverless computing.

    Suggestions/Recommendations:

  • GitHub Pages: works well for static sites
  • Heroku: a PaaS more suited to full-stack applications great for proof-of concept or quick demos; gets expensive if trying to run anything production ready.
  • Digital Ocean: is a cloud provider. Their range of virtual private server (VPS) options gives you the choice of pre-built solutions including bloging and e-commerce or you can install your own stack and applications from thro/s upwards.

A Domain is ...

... the name for a location on the internet. Computers use numbers in the form of ip (=Internet Protocol) address such as 12.34.56.78 to communicate over the internet. 'The Domain Name System (DNS) is the phonebook of the internet.' [cloudflare.com]

  • DNS searches: first the local cache is searched to see if the resource is already known. If not then a chain of searches takes place that ultimately results in an ip address being returned to the application on your computer that wants to communicate over the 'net.
  • Structure of a domain your domain consists of a name you choose like the name of your town, business or blog (the bit before the DOT) plus top level domains (TLDs) such as .com or .org.

GitHub Pages ...

... is a hosting service available to anyone with a GitHub account. It is ideal for any kind of static site such a project documentation or a simple blog.
It can be used to host html and css and compiled ReactJS apps. Pages can also be written in Markdown.
Pages can be uploaded by git or by drag and drop from your local file browser in to the GHP page in your [web]browser.
